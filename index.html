    Body & Calm

    Gentle care for your nervous system, one breath at a time.

    🤸 Gentle Movement Library

    Choose what your body needs right now.

    🌅
    Full Body Stretch2 min · Very gentle
    ▾
    Lie flat on your back. Reach both arms above your head. Point your toes. Breathe in slowly for 5 counts as you stretch long. Hold at the top, then release everything on the exhale. Repeat 3×.
    🤗
    Knee Hug1–2 min · Grounding
    ▾
    Pull both knees gently to your chest. Wrap your arms around your shins. Rock slowly side to side to massage your lower back. Breathe deeply. Stay here as long as feels good.
    🦋
    Butterfly Knees2 min · Hip opening
    ▾
    Bring the soles of your feet together. Let your knees fall open like butterfly wings. Place hands on your belly. Breathe into the openness. Let gravity do the work — no forcing.
    🌀
    Supine Twist3 min · Spine release
    ▾
    Lie on your back. Bend your right knee and let it fall across your body to the left. Stretch your right arm out wide. Look toward your right hand. Hold 30–60 sec each side. Breathe into the twist.
    🦒
    Neck Rolls2 min · Tension release
    ▾
    Sitting or lying. Slowly drop your right ear toward your right shoulder. Hold for 5 breaths. Roll chin to chest. Move to the left side. Go slowly. Never force. Let the weight of your head do the work.
    💃
    Seated March3 min · Energising
    ▾
    Sit in a chair with feet flat on the floor. Lift your right knee, then left, in a slow marching motion. Let your arms swing naturally. Start slow, then find your own rhythm. Great for mornings when standing feels like too much.
    🌊
    Sway & Shift2 min · Calming
    ▾
    Stand with feet hip-width apart. Gently shift your weight from one foot to the other. Let your arms float. Add a soft sway if it feels good. This gentle rhythmic movement soothes the nervous system.
    🙌
    Overhead Reach2 min · Opening
    ▾
    Standing or seated. Raise both arms overhead. Reach right, then left, creating a gentle side bend. Breathe into the stretch. Lower and repeat. Wakes up the side body without strain.
    🚶
    Slow Mindful Walk5 min · Grounding
    ▾
    Walk very slowly — slower than you think. Feel each heel-to-toe connection with the floor. Coordinate your breath: inhale for 3 steps, exhale for 3 steps. The goal is presence, not distance.
    🧘
    Seated Forward Fold2 min · Releasing
    ▾
    Sit at the edge of a chair. Hinge forward from your hips (not your waist). Let your head hang heavy. Let your arms dangle. Breathe. This position activates the parasympathetic nervous system.
    🔄
    Shoulder Rolls1 min · Tension melt
    ▾
    Roll both shoulders slowly backward 8 times, then forward 8 times. Exaggerate the movement — really lift them toward your ears on the way up and let them drop on the way down. Exhale on every drop.
    🤸
    Hip Circles2 min · Hip opening
    ▾
    Stand with hands on hips. Draw a big slow circle with your pelvis — forward, side, back, side. 8 circles one direction, then reverse. Your upper body stays relatively still. Let it feel a little silly.
    🦶
    Ankle Circles2 min · Circulation
    ▾
    Seated. Lift one foot off the floor. Draw 8 slow circles clockwise, then counterclockwise. Point and flex the foot 5 times. Switch sides. Helps with circulation and body awareness.
    🤲
    Wrist & Hand Release1 min · Gentle
    ▾
    Shake your hands loose for 10 seconds. Then make slow circles with each wrist. Spread your fingers wide, then curl them in. Repeat 5×. Especially good if you spend time on screens or clench when anxious.
    🫁
    Rib Cage Breathing2 min · Expansion
    ▾
    Place both hands on the sides of your ribs. As you inhale, try to push your hands apart sideways — like an accordion opening. Exhale and feel the ribs fall. This unlocks stuck tension in the thoracic spine.
    📦
    Box Breathing4 min · Regulating
    ▾
    Inhale through your nose for 4 counts. Hold for 4. Exhale through your mouth for 4. Hold empty for 4. Repeat 4–8 rounds. Used by Navy SEALs and therapists alike — it balances your nervous system quickly.
    🔢
    4-7-8 Breath3 min · Sleep & calm
    ▾
    Inhale quietly through your nose for 4. Hold your breath for 7. Exhale completely through your mouth for 8 counts making a whooshing sound. This is one of the most effective techniques for anxiety and pre-sleep calm.
    🌊
    Ocean Breath2 min · Soothing
    ▾
    Breathe in deeply through your nose. Exhale slowly through a slightly open mouth with a soft "haaa" sound — like fogging a mirror. The sound itself activates your vagus nerve and signals safety to your brain.
    💜
    Belly Breathing3 min · Grounding
    ▾
    Place one hand on your belly, one on your chest. Breathe so only the belly hand rises. Chest stays still. Inhale 4, exhale 6. This activates the diaphragm and tells your nervous system it is safe to rest.
    👃
    Alternate Nostril3 min · Balancing
    ▾
    Close your right nostril with your thumb and inhale left for 4. Close both, hold for 2. Open right, exhale for 4. Inhale right for 4. Close, hold. Exhale left. That's one round. Do 5 rounds. Balances both hemispheres of the brain.
